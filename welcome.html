A quick note before we begin

YomiSaver works directly with the Japanese text on webpages you visit. Before enabling it, here is exactly what it does with that information.

01

Page text is processed locally

YomiSaver reads Japanese text on standard HTTP and HTTPS webpages so it can add furigana and identify words.

Tokenisation and furigana generation happen inside your browser.

02

Dictionary terms use Jisho

When you request a definition, the Japanese word being looked up is sent securely to Jisho.org.

JLPT enrichment may also send individual Japanese vocabulary terms when local level data is unavailable.

03

Saved cards stay in Chrome

Saved vocabulary can include the word, reading, meanings, sentence context, page title, and page URL.

Cards are stored in Chrome’s local extension storage. Display preferences may sync through Chrome if browser sync is enabled.

04

No tracking or advertising

YomiSaver has no user account, analytics, behavioural tracking, or advertising.

Your saved vocabulary is not sold or used to build a profile about you.

What enabling YomiSaver means

By selecting “Enable YomiSaver,” you allow the extension to:

  • Process Japanese text on webpages to provide reading assistance.
  • Send dictionary lookup terms to Jisho.org over HTTPS.
  • Store vocabulary cards and their source context in Chrome’s extension storage.
  • Store your display and JLPT preferences using Chrome storage.

YomiSaver uses this information only to provide its Japanese reading and vocabulary features.

YomiSaver remains paused until you choose to enable it.
